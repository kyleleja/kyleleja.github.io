Well first off let's examine their similarities. Lightbot may look different on the outside, but
internally it’s basically is a computer, be it a heavily simplified one, but one nonetheless. For
example, Lightbot is in a set state, and different inputs, (actions), change that state in a variety
of ways, like if it “moved forward” it would change position, and if it turned it would change its
direction, and that new change becoming the new state. A computer does the same thing, it’s
just infinitely more complex, like typing on keyboard changes different elements and these
changes are incorporated into the new state. As for how Lightbot differs from a computer, the
solution isn't as clear. For one there is the complexity difference, while Lightbot is restricted to
certain inputs, like the move command, and specified outputs like turning or moving Lightbot.
Computers aren't as restrictive, they have almost an infinite mix of inputs and outputs. These
functions can even be adapted. Furthermore, normal computers can have artificial intelligence
and can connect to a web of other computers that span the globe, while Lightbot is restricted to
its resources, and doesn’t have A.I.
